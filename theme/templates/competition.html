A Conceptual Design for a 100-Year Autonomous Kuiper Belt Colony for 200,000 Residents

About the Project

"Modern Island" a large-scale, self-sustaining orbital colony in the Kuiper Belt, designed to house a permanent, independently growing population of up to 200,000 residents. The primary objective is to achieve complete resource autonomy from Earth for a minimum operational period of 100 years. The habitat is designed as a modular, rotating O'Neill-style cylinder complex, providing artificial gravity at approximately 0.8g (80% of Earth's gravity) to mitigate the long-term health risks of microgravity

Teams must address the engineering, biological, and demographic models underpinning the Modern Island concept, demonstrating its feasibility as a permanent, autonomous human outpost in the outer solar system

The Kuiper Belt Frontier

Kuiper Belt Objects (KBOs) are pristine reservoirs of water ice, ammonia, methane, nitrogen, and complex organics-the fundamental building blocks for life, propellant, and industrial manufacturing
